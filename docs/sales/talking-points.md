# Soliplex White-Label Theming — Talking Points

Quick-reference card for calls and demos. Lead with the headline, support with
proof, close with the business impact. Skim before the call; glance during it.

---

## The one-liner

> "One platform, every customer's brand, zero forks — branding is now a config
> object, not an engineering project."

## Three headline messages

1. **Their brand, their product.** Customers run the full Soliplex experience
   under their own colors, fonts, and light/dark modes.
2. **Repeatable, near-zero marginal cost.** A new brand is configuration in
   hours — not a forked codebase maintained forever.
3. **Production-grade and future-proof.** Tested, documented, accessible,
   cross-platform, built on Material 3, designed to scale across customers.

---

## Capability points (what it does)

- Customer supplies a handful of brand colors → system generates a complete,
  balanced color system (200+ values) for **both light and dark** automatically.
- Name any font — bundled fonts load instantly, others fetch and cache at
  runtime. No rebuild.
- **40+ UI component types** themed centrally → the brand lands everywhere,
  consistently, including future screens.
- Dark mode built in: remembered between sessions, one-tap toggle.
- Polished default theme ships out of the box — never a "broken until
  configured" state.
- Responsive across phone, tablet, and desktop; runs on web, iOS, macOS,
  Android.

## Proof points (why it's real)

- Dedicated automated tests for color, fonts, persistence, and the toggle.
- 80% test-coverage bar enforced in continuous integration.
- Two engineering docs: full architecture + a step-by-step setup guide →
  transferable, repeatable.
- Built on Material 3, the current industry-standard design language.
- Includes a device-level reliability fix found in real testing.

## Business impact (why it matters)

- **For the customer:** ownership and trust, accessibility engineered in,
  one identity across every platform, modern dark mode standard.
- **For us:** one codebase not N forks, new brands in hours not weeks, branding
  sold as a feature not a service, predictable low-risk delivery.

---

## Discovery questions to ask

- "How important is it that the product reflects your brand to your end users?"
- "Do your users expect dark mode? Accessibility compliance?"
- "Are you rolling this out to one team, or across business units / clients with
  different identities?"
- "Have past vendors charged you — in time or money — for custom branding?"
- "Who owns brand guidelines on your side, and how strict are they?"

## Objection handling

| Objection | Response |
| --------- | -------- |
| "Custom branding sounds expensive / slow." | "That's exactly what we fixed. It's now a configuration input — hours, not a project. We can often show your colors live on this call." |
| "Will it actually be consistent everywhere?" | "Yes — 40+ component types are themed from one central place, so the brand applies across the whole app automatically, including screens we haven't built yet." |
| "What about accessibility / compliance?" | "Text colors are computed for correct contrast, controls carry proper accessibility labels, and the layout adapts responsively. It's engineered in, not bolted on." |
| "We need dark mode." | "Built in. Generated from the same brand colors, remembered between sessions, toggled with one tap." |
| "Is this a prototype or real?" | "Production-grade: dedicated automated tests, 80% coverage in CI, full documentation, and validated on real devices." |
| "We have very specific fonts." | "Name them. Bundled fonts load instantly; any other font is fetched and cached at runtime — no rebuild needed." |
| "We're rolling out to many sub-brands." | "That's the sweet spot. Same codebase, each brand is just configuration — scale without multiplying cost." |

## Multi-customer / strategic angle

- Scale from 10 to 1,000 customers on **one codebase** — growth doesn't multiply
  engineering cost.
- Channel / OEM / reseller ready — partners ship under their own or clients'
  identities.
- Tiered packaging: default look at entry, full custom palette + brand fonts at
  enterprise.
- Every fix and feature reaches every customer the instant it ships — no
  back-porting, no drift.

## Close

> "We turned branding from a per-customer cost into a product capability. That
> means we can make it look exactly like you — fast — and everything we build
> from here lands in your brand automatically. Want to see your colors in it?"
